About This Website

My name is Marley Gonsalves and I am a first year Master’s student at the University of New Hampshire. I am currently being advised by Dr. Liz Harvey and Dr. Brittany Jellison.

My primary research focus is phytoplankton ecology, and I am particularly interested in how it is impacted by human activity. For my current project I will be investigating the correlation between phytoplankton community composition and the feeding behavior of Eastern Oysters in Great Bay, New Hampshire. I will be taking regular samples and analyzing them, looking at nutrient contents (lipids, carbohydrates, and calories) and species present, and how this changes over time. I will then be comparing this with data from oysters at the sample site, which will tell me when the oysters are feeding. In theory, the oysters will be feeding more when there is good food in the area, or when the phytoplankton present are suitable for them. I hope to use this research to try and see if the phytoplankton present in Great Bay are suitable for oyster aquaculture, while also analyzing phytoplankton nutrient profiles over time.
